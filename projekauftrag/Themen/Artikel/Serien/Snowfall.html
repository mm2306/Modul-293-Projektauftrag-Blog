Snowfall

Crime Drama Creators: John Singleton, Eric Amadio, & Dave Andron

"Snowfall" takes viewers back to the summer of 1983 in Los Angeles, chronicling the very beginning of the crack cocaine epidemic. The story is told through the eyes of several characters whose lives are on a violent collision course. At the center is Franklin Saint, a young, ambitious street entrepreneur who sees an opportunity to rise above his circumstances by entering the drug trade.

Starting as a small-time dealer, Franklin's intelligence and drive quickly propel him up the ranks, building a powerful and far-reaching criminal empire. The series also follows other key players, including a CIA operative with a dark agenda, a Mexican luchador caught in a power struggle with a crime family, and the local residents whose lives are forever changed by the drug's destructive impact on their community.

Created by the legendary director John Singleton, "Snowfall" is a gritty, intense, and character-driven drama. It masterfully depicts the complex social and political factors that fueled the epidemic while telling a compelling story of ambition, power, and consequence. The show is a must-see for fans of crime sagas like "The Wire" and "Narcos."

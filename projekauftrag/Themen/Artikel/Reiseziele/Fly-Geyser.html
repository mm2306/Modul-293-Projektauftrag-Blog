Fly Geyser

United States June 11, 2025

Fly Geyser is a small geothermal geyser located on a private ranch in the Black Rock Desert of Nevada. What makes it so remarkable is that it wasn't formed entirely by nature; it's a fascinating example of an accidental human creation transformed by natural processes. It was created in 1964 after a geothermal energy company drilled a test well, which was then improperly sealed, allowing dissolved minerals to rise and accumulate.

Over the decades, this accumulation of calcium carbonate has formed a large, cone-shaped mound, or travertin, that stands about 1.5 meters (5 ft) tall and continues to grow. Hot water constantly erupts from multiple spouts, creating terraces and pools on the desert floor. The geyser's brilliant red, green, and orange colors are not from paint, but from thermophilic algae that thrive in the moist, hot environment.

The geyser is located on private land, the Fly Ranch, which was purchased by the Burning Man Project in 2016. Access is restricted to guided nature walks, making it an exclusive and mysterious destination. This blend of accidental art and natural wonder makes Fly Geyser a unique spectacle and a goal for those who seek the weird and wonderful.

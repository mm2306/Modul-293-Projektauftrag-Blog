Socotra Island

Yemen June 7, 2025

Socotra is an island archipelago in the Indian Ocean, belonging to Yemen. Its extreme isolation and harsh, dry climate have resulted in a unique and spectacular endemic flora. It is so unique, in fact, that it's often described as the "most alien-looking place on Earth."

The island is most famous for the Dragon's Blood Tree (Dracaena cinnabari), a strange, umbrella-shaped tree that produces a dark red resin, known as "dragon's blood." This resin has been used for centuries in medicine, incense, and as a dye. Along with the Dragon's Blood Tree, Socotra is home to hundreds of other endemic plant species, including the bizarre Bottle Tree (Adenium obesum socotranum).

Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site for its "outstanding universal value" due to its incredible biodiversity, visiting Socotra is a dream for adventurers, botanists, and anyone seeking a truly off-the-beaten-path experience. It represents a journey to a landscape that feels like a remnant of a prehistoric world.

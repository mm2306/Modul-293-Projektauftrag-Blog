Salar de Uyuni

Bolivia June 3, 2025

Salar de Uyuni is the world's largest salt flat, located in the Daniel Campos Province in Potos√≠ in southwest Bolivia. It is the world's largest salt flat at 10,582 square kilometers (4,086 sq mi). It's a landscape of astonishing, almost alien beauty.

Formed as a result of transformations between several prehistoric lakes, it is covered by a few meters of salt crust, which has an extraordinary flatness. When it's covered by a thin layer of water (usually during the wet season from December to April), it becomes a giant, stunning mirror reflecting the sky.

Beyond the main salt flat, the region is home to striking geological formations, active volcanoes, and unique wildlife like flamingos in high-altitude lagoons. Visiting requires careful planning due to the remote location and harsh conditions, but the reward is an unforgettable experience of vastness and natural wonder.

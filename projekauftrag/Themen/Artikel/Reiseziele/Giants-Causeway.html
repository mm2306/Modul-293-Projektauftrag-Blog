Giant's Causeway

Northern Ireland June 5, 2025

Located on the coast of County Antrim in Northern Ireland, the Giant's Causeway is an area of about 40,000 interlocking basalt columns, the result of an ancient volcanic fissure eruption. It was declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 1986.

The tops of the columns form stepping stones that lead from the cliff foot and disappear under the sea. Most of the columns are hexagonal, although there are also some with four, five, seven or eight sides. The tallest are about 12 metres (39 ft) high, and the solidified lava in the cliffs is 28 metres (92 ft) thick in places.

Beyond the incredible geology, the site is steeped in myth and legend. The story goes that the causeway was built by the Irish giant Fionn mac Cumhaill (Finn MacCool) as a path to cross the sea to fight the Scottish giant Benandonner. This blend of natural wonder and folklore makes it a truly captivating destination.

Moraine Lake

Canada June 9, 2025

Moraine Lake is a glacially fed lake nestled in Banff National Park, Alberta, Canada. Situated in the majestic Valley of the Ten Peaks at an elevation of about 1,885 meters (6,183 feet), it is one of the most iconic and photographed locations in the entire Canadian Rockies.

The lake's stunning, vivid shade of turquoise is its most famous feature. The color is typically at its most intense and brilliant in mid-to-late summer when the glaciers have melted sufficiently.

Visitors can experience its beauty from various perspectives. The most famous viewpoint is from the top of the Rockpile Trail, a short but rewarding climb that offers the classic vista seen on postcards and banknotes. For the more adventurous, several hiking trails start from the lake, leading into the pristine alpine wilderness of the Valley of the Ten Peaks. It is, without a doubt, a bucket-list destination for any nature lover.
